title: Managing downloads

---

The Downloads page provides complete control over your active and completed downloads. Monitor progress, pause and resume downloads, retry failed items, and manage your download history.

## Download states

Downloads can be in one of these states:

- **Downloading**: Currently being downloaded
- **Waiting**: Queued and waiting to start (when concurrent download limit is reached)
- **Paused**: Temporarily stopped by user
- **Completed**: Successfully downloaded
- **Failed**: Download encountered an error

## Viewing downloads

The Downloads page displays three categories:

1. **Running downloads**: Currently active downloads with real-time progress
2. **Queued downloads**: Waiting to start based on concurrent download limits
3. **Download history**: All completed, failed, and paused downloads

The header shows counts for each category, for example: Downloads (15) (2) (3) indicates 15 historical downloads, 2 running, and 3 queued.

## Download progress information

Each active download displays:

- **Thumbnail**: Preview image with video duration
- **Title**: Media title
- **Uploader**: Channel or account name
- **Format**: Selected quality and format (e.g., "1080p - mp4")
- **Time range**: If downloading a section, shows start and end times
- **Progress bar**: Visual indicator with color coding:
  - Blue: Downloading
  - Yellow: Waiting/Queued
  - Gray: Paused
  - Red: Failed
  - Green: Completed
- **Progress details**: Download speed, file size, ETA, and percentage

## Controlling individual downloads

### Pause a download

<Steps>
  <Step title="Locate the download">
    Find the download in the running or queued section.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Click pause button">
    Click the pause icon in the download card's footer. The download will stop and move to a paused state.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
You can pause both active downloads and queued downloads. Paused downloads remain in your history until you resume or delete them.
</Note>

### Resume a download

<Steps>
  <Step title="Find paused download">
    Paused downloads appear in the download history with a gray progress bar.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Click resume button">
    Click the play icon to resume the download. It will either start immediately or enter the queue based on your concurrent download settings.
  </Step>
</Steps>

### Retry a failed download

When a download fails due to network issues or other errors:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Identify failed download">
    Failed downloads show a red progress bar and error status.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Click retry button">
    Click the retry icon (circular arrow) to attempt the download again.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
If a download repeatedly fails, check your internet connection or try selecting a different format. Some formats may not be available anymore.
</Tip>

## Bulk download operations

The options menu (three-dot icon in the header) provides bulk actions:

### Pause all downloads

Stops all currently running downloads at once. Use this when you need to:
- Free up bandwidth temporarily
- Prevent interference with other network activities
- Stop downloads before closing the application

### Pause waiting downloads

Pauses only the downloads in the queue, while allowing active downloads to complete.

### Resume paused downloads

Restarts all paused downloads. They will either begin immediately or enter the queue based on available slots.

### Retry failed downloads

Attempts to restart all failed downloads simultaneously. This is useful after resolving network issues or when multiple downloads failed at once.

<Warning>
Retrying many failed downloads at once may queue most of them based on your concurrent download limit.
</Warning>

## Queue management

VidArchive automatically manages download queues based on your **maximum concurrent downloads** setting:

1. Up to the configured number of downloads run simultaneously (default: 2)
2. Additional downloads automatically enter the waiting state
3. When an active download completes or is paused, the next queued download starts
4. You can configure up to 5 concurrent downloads in Settings

<Accordion title="Why limit concurrent downloads?">
  Limiting concurrent downloads helps:
  - Prevent bandwidth saturation
  - Maintain stable download speeds
  - Reduce system resource usage
  - Ensure downloads complete more reliably

  Most users achieve better overall performance with 2-3 concurrent downloads rather than downloading many files simultaneously.
</Accordion>

## Download history

Completed and failed downloads remain in your history, providing:

### Viewing completed downloads

Each completed download card shows:
- Full metadata (title, uploader, format, duration)
- Download completion status
- Quick actions to access the file

### Actions for completed downloads

- **Show in folder**: Opens the file location in your system file browser
- **Play in default player**: Opens the media file with your system's default player
- **Play**: Opens the built-in media player within VidArchive
- **Delete from history**: Removes the entry from your history (doesn't delete the file)

<Info>
Deleting an item from history only removes the record from VidArchive. The downloaded file remains on your system.
</Info>

### Searching download history

Use the search box in the Downloads header to:

1. Type any part of the title, uploader, or URL
2. Results filter in real-time
3. Clear the search to see all downloads again

### Clearing download history

To remove all entries from download history:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open options menu">
    Click the three-dot menu icon in the Downloads header.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Select delete history">
    Choose "Delete download history" from the menu.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Confirm deletion">
    Confirm the action in the dialog that appears.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Warning>
This action cannot be undone. Your downloaded files will not be deleted, but you'll lose the history records and metadata.
</Warning>

## Accessing media details

For any download (active, completed, or failed), you can:

1. Click the **info icon** to view detailed metadata
2. Click the **thumbnail** to navigate back to the media info page
3. Click the **external link** icon to open the original URL in your browser
4. Click the **photo icon** to view the thumbnail image in your browser
